\section{Skills}
\subsection{Research and Analysis}
Through doctoral studies at Newcastle University I trained in advanced quantitative methods. I have extensive data experience, applying regression models (including GAMMs) to language data to answer complex research questions, as reflected in my thesis and multiple peer-reviewed publications. I provide statistical support to a broad cross-section of researchers and am able to understand and advise on new statistical methods under pressure.

\subsection{Software and Coding}
Throughout my academic roles at both Newcastle and Manchester Metropolitan Universities, I have kept on top of emerging software and techniques in the field and assessed the suitability for various research challenges. I have used R for data analysis for over 8 years. I have worked with and provided training on command line software, Python programmes and proprietary software scripting in unfamiliar languages. I have used git for version tracking, branch control, and issue tracking.

\subsection{Teaching and Training}
I bring over five years of experience in higher education, having crafted and delivered lectures, seminars, and workshops at an undergraduate level and designed and run a postgraduate level module in research methods and quantitative analysis using R. My role as a lab manager at Newcastle University included one-to-one and group mentoring, where I advised and supported students and staff in experimental and statistical methods and my roles at both Newcastle and Manchester Metropolitan University have involved training and supervising research assistants where I have communicated complex technical concepts to students including transferring skills that they could take forwards into their own ongoing work.
